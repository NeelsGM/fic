Why Forged Carbon?

Traditional "Woven" carbon fiber is restricted by the direction of the weave. While strong, it struggles with complex 3D geometries and sharp internal corners.

Isotropic Strength: Strength in all directions, not just the weave path.
Complex Geometry: Ideal for intake manifolds, brackets, and aero-fins.
Unique Aesthetic: A marbled, high-tech finish unique to every part.

The Manufacturing Edge

By utilizing 3D printed sacrificial tooling and compression molding, we produce parts that bridge the gap between expensive aerospace composites and heavy metal components. This is the Moon Shot of local manufacturing: aerospace materials, produced with rapid-iteration speed.

Our Ecosystem

Virtual Engineering

We use advanced CAD software to design parts optimized for additive manufacturing. This includes generative design, weight reduction, and ensuring sub-millimeter fitment for automotive applications.

Industrial Printing

Utilizing high-temp polymers and carbon-infused filaments (PA12-CF), we print functional parts that can withstand heat, chemicals, and mechanical stress. These aren't prototypes; they are end-use components.

Precision Scanning

Have a broken or discontinued part? We scan physical objects to create digital "Master Files." This allows for perfect replication or reverse engineering of complex mechanical assemblies.
